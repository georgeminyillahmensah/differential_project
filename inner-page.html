    Modified Model

    Introduction

    The ecosyestem will never be balanced if there was no predation. Predation has been the major reason why there is balance in the ecosystem

    Numerous mathematical models have been developed and these models have become essential tools in analyzing the interaction of different populations,particularly the interaction between prey and predator.

    These Models were created based on some assumptions and below are some of these models.

    • Hadeler and Freedman considered the prey-predator model in which predation is more likely on infected prey.
    • Anderson and May discussed a model where the influence of epidemics on predation was first studied.
    • Haque and Venturino discussed models of diseases spreading in symbiotic communities.

    relevance of the paper

    Parasite infection in the prey and predator populations is an issue which cannot be ignored, and also it has an important role in regulating population sizes. There are numerous field evidences where the prey and the predator are infected by parasites.Thus, reality dictates that, we should be concerned with prey-predator systems, where both the populations are disease affected.

    Description of the model

    Below are some of the Assumptions for this model

    1. Only the susceptible prey can reproduce. They further assume that the predator population have a natural death, whereas the infected population have a disease induce excess death rate also.

    2. In the absence of predator population and with no disease, the prey population grows logistically with intrinsic growth rate r and environmental carrying capacity K(K > 0).

    The disease spreads among the susceptible prey when it comes in contact with the infected one. The prey, once became infected, never recovers. It will either die or will be removed by predation

    In presence of the disease in prey, the infected prey population contributes to the susceptible prey population growth towards the carrying capacity K(K > 0).

    The infected predators are unfit to be able to catch a healthy prey, and as such a healthy prey is caught by a healthy predator only. But an infected prey, being weak and more vulnerable, is available for predation by both susceptible and infected predators

    The disease spread over predator population by direct contact with an infected predator. An infected predator never becomes recovered or immune–it remains infected or dies out.

    This model consists of two populations: the prey population and the predator population. Both the populations have two sub classes: susceptible and infected. At time T, let S(T) denotes the density of the susceptible prey, and I(T) denotes the density of the infected prey. The susceptible and infected predator densities are denoted by X(T) and Y(T), respectively. Using the assumptions above,the paper came out with the following parameters

    the parameter a1 is the infection rate for prey population, b1 is the predation rate of susceptible prey by healthy predators. The infected prey population has a disease induced death d1. f1 and m1 are respectively the predation rate of infected prey by susceptible and infected predators. The parameters c1 and 11 are the conversion rates for healthy and infected preys to healthy predator. δ1 is the natural death rate for the predator population. The infected predator population has a disease induced death rate α1. We have taken e1 as the infection rate for predator population and n1 as the conversion rate for infected prey to infected predator. We make an obvious assumption that all the parameters are positive.

    Implications

    From the graphs seen above, the prey population increases food for predators. However, the increase in the infected population moderates the population of the susceptible prey and predator.This relationship continues in different waves until an equillibrium point is reached. The function E(s*,i*,x*,y*) represents that point at which all the populations are constant. At the equilibrium point, the rate at which susceptible prey become infected and also infected preys die will be constant. The same will happen for the predator population. And the natural death rate will also be constant.Therefore the ecosystem will be stable will the rate of death for both populations and, the growth rate and also the rate of infection will be constant throughout.
